## ICP 1: THE SPRINT (Founder GTM Sprint)

### Firmographics
- **Revenue:** $600K–$1.2M ARR ($50K–$100K MRR). OR raised >$2M recently (seed/pre-seed). Sweet spot: $50–$100K MRR or freshly funded.
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS, dev tools, fintech, AI/ML infrastructure, vertical SaaS. Product companies only — not services.
- **Employees:** 1–10. Buyer has nobody doing marketing.
- **Location:** North America, English-speaking. Time zone overlap required (coaching calls in real time).

### Technographics
- **Complementary:** LinkedIn, any CRM (HubSpot free, Pipedrive, Close), Calendly, podcast platforms.
- **Blocking:** Existing agency retainer, existing FT marketing hire, founder not willing to use LinkedIn.
- **Sophistication:** Working product + some customers. Pre-product = disqualified.
- **Current spend:** $0–$2K/month scattered (Canva, Buffer, occasional freelancer). No system.

### Qualifying Characteristics
- **Users:** 1–2 (founder + maybe co-founder)
- **Buyer/Champion:** Founder/CEO always. Same-week close if fit is right.
- **Budget replaces:** Freelance content ($500–1,500/mo) + tools ($50–200/mo) + podcast booking ($500–1,000/mo) + founder time opportunity cost ($2,000–5,000/mo) = $3,000–8,000/mo → consolidated at $3K/mo.
- **Pricing inhibitors:** Below $50K MRR without recent funding: $3K feels like a big bet. Above $100K MRR: questions quality. Recently raised >$2M: $3K is noise — close fast.

### Accelerators (buying signal triggers)
- Just raised seed round
- Competitor getting press — urgency fresh
- Launched product to crickets
- Posting inconsistently while watching worse-product competitors win
- Just lost deal to unknown competitor

### Readiness to Buy Signals
- Posting for marketing hire / content person (intercept before $60–80K salary commitment)
- Just closed seed/pre-seed — board asking about GTM
- Posting on LinkedIn sporadically (once a week or less)
- Commenting on competitors' posts
- Asking in Slack/Twitter about founder-led content tools
- Recently changed LinkedIn headline or company page copy

### Disqualifiers
- No product yet
- Not willing to appear on LinkedIn or podcasts
- Already has marketing team or agency
- B2C company
- Wants sales calls done for them
- Commission-only / no retainer

### Persona: "Alex"
Technical co-founder, B2B SaaS, $35K MRR, 40 paying customers, word-of-mouth growth. Posts LinkedIn twice a month. Competitor with inferior product just landed a podcast feature. Has $3K/month if proven. Decides in one meeting.

## ICP 2: AI WORKFORCE (Core — Insurance & Financial Services)

### Firmographics
- **Revenue:** $1M–$10M annual.
- **Industry:**
  - Tier 1: P&C insurance brokerages, independent financial advisory, wealth management
  - Tier 2: Mortgage brokerages, benefits consulting, commercial RE brokerages
  - Tier 3: Accounting firms, law firms
- **Employees:** 5–30.
- **Location:** Primary: Ontario, Canada. Secondary: Greater Canada. Tertiary: US.

### Technographics
- **Complementary:** Redtail, Wealthbox, AMS360, Applied Epic, HubSpot, Outlook, Gmail, Google Workspace.
- **Blocking:** Fully closed CRM (no API/export), existing AI automation vendor deployed, compliance platforms blocking external integration.
- **Sophistication:** Uses a CRM (even inconsistently), has email + website + some LinkedIn. Doesn't need to be technical.
- **Current spend:** $2–5K/mo marketing agency + $2–4K/mo part-time admin + $50–200/mo/user CRM. Or spending nothing and losing clients.

### Qualifying Characteristics
- **Users:** 2–10 per firm.
- **Buyer/Champion:** Principal/Owner/Managing Partner. Top-down sale — deal stalls without principal in room.
- **Budget replaces:** Marketing agency ($2–5K/mo) + admin partial salary ($35–55K/yr) + CRM consultant ($5–15K/yr) + lead gen tools ($500–2K/mo).
- **Pricing inhibitors:** Below $1M: can't justify $3–5K/mo. Above $10M: expects enterprise SLAs.

### Accelerators (buying signal triggers)
- Lost significant client to competitor who stayed in touch
- 30%+ of book not contacted in 6+ months
- Failed hire
- Regulatory audit exposing communication gaps
- Renewal season approaching with no plan
- Competitor visibly growing through content

### Readiness to Buy Signals
- Posting for admin, marketing coordinator, or junior broker
- Just acquired another firm's book of business (capacity crisis)
- Searching for AI tools for insurance / attending InsurTech events
- Engaging with AI content on LinkedIn
- Asking peers about client retention tools
- New website or CRM implementation in progress
- Expanding into new lines / opening second office

### Disqualifiers
- No CRM + unwilling to adopt one
- Ideologically opposed to AI
- Active regulatory trouble
- Fewer than 100 active client relationships
- Decision-maker is not the principal
- Wants one-time project
- Completely closed tech stack

### Persona: "Sarah"
Owner, 12-person insurance brokerage, SW Ontario, ~$3M revenue, 430 client relationships. Uses Redtail inconsistently. One overwhelmed admin. Lost notable account last quarter — no follow-up. Budget $1,500–3,000/mo if clear ROI within 90 days.
