04 / How it's built

Built where it matters.
Bought where it doesn't.

Modern applications combine custom-built logic with proven infrastructure. We're building the chamber-specific functionality custom, while running it on the same trusted services that power thousands of professional applications. This keeps build costs lower, reliability higher, and the chamber from depending on us for things the world has already solved.

Custom-built for the chamber

The CRM. Member directory. Event creation and registration. Member self-service portal. Engagement reporting. The website itself. Admin dashboard. All built specifically for our workflow.

Trusted infrastructure (integrated)

Zeffy for payments and event registration (free for nonprofits, no processor fees). Payload or Directus for member-authored content with moderation. Cloudflare for hosting and security. Resend for transactional email. Mailchimp for newsletters. Google Analytics 4 for traffic data.

Why this matters

Zeffy is purpose-built for Canadian nonprofits and chambers, with no processor fees passed to the chamber. Payload and Directus are open-source content platforms used by serious teams who need flexibility, not template-based editors. The chamber gets professional-grade tools at the cost of a custom application, without depending on a single vendor for everything.

07 / Custom-built vs off-the-shelf

Off-the-shelf has real strengths.
Here's how custom answers each one.

A fair comparison gives the off-the-shelf platform credit where it earns it, then shows how this proposal addresses each strength directly.

Off-The-Shelf Strength

Dedicated support team and help center.

Hundreds of chambers in their support queue. Comprehensive knowledge base. 24/7 ticketing.

→
Custom Answer

Direct line to the person who built it.

No ticket queues. No tier-1 support gatekeeping. The chamber gets the same person responding to questions who designed and wrote the system.

Off-The-Shelf Strength

Pre-built integrations with chamber-specific tools.

Out-of-the-box connections to event platforms, accounting software, and member services.

→
Custom Answer

Built-in integrations to the tools we actually use.

Zeffy for payments. Mailchimp for newsletters. Cloudflare for hosting. The integrations that matter to the chamber, set up correctly the first time.

Off-The-Shelf Strength

Established product roadmap and ongoing updates.

New features released to all customers periodically.

→
Custom Answer

The roadmap is whatever the chamber decides.

25 hours of new features per year, included. Built when the chamber needs them, not when a vendor gets around to releasing them. Features serve our chamber, not 500 others.

Off-The-Shelf Strength

Larger user community across hundreds of chambers.

Forums, peer chamber support, shared best practices.

→
Custom Answer

Documentation written for our chamber.

Plain-language guides built around how our staff actually uses the system. Not a generic help center the chamber president has to translate every time she has a question.

The bigger picture

Off-the-shelf platforms are built to serve hundreds of chambers reasonably well. This proposal is built to serve one chamber exceptionally well, at roughly half the 5-year cost. Both are valid choices. The board's job is to decide which approach fits the chamber's culture, budget, and direction.
