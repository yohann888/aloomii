What the Agents Do

The agents handle the volume work that used to eat 40 hours a week: monitoring for buying signals, surfacing warm intro paths, drafting content, flagging fading relationships, and matching you to events where your prospects show up.

They run 24/7. Self-hosted. Your data never leaves.

Every output gets reviewed by a human before it ships. The last 20%, tone, timing, relationship nuance, stays human. That's the part tools can't do and agencies don't bother with.

Self-Use Case Study

We ran this on ourselves first.

Month 1: 0 podcast bookings. Ran Signal Scout + Content Engine.

Month 2: Jim Rogers (Quantum Fund co-founder) said yes to Pale Blue Nexus. 17-year relationship nurtured by agents.

Month 3: 6 founder intros. 2 meetings booked. 1 partnership discussion.

12 qualified conversations total. Exactly as promised.

The BD 2.0 Job Description

Role: AI Agent Manager (BD 2.0)

Team: 1-10 person company, $50K-$100K MRR

Salary: $145K → $3,500/month (fleet cost)


Daily Tasks:

- Review 15 agent outputs (30 min)

- Approve 3 touchpoints (LinkedIn DMs, emails)

- Monitor signal cockpit (10 min)

- Adjust agent configs (5 min/week)


Requirements:

- Know your ICP pains

- Spot signal from noise

- Human judgment on relationship nuance


No coding. No 80-hour weeks. 1-2 hours/week total.

Why This Works

Tools fail at the last 20%. Tone. Timing. When to push, when to wait.

Agencies charge $15K/month and churn at 60 days.

We charge $3,500. Human review on every output. Clients stay.
