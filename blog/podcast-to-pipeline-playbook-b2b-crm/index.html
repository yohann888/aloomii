TL;DR

The podcast conversation is just the beginning of the relationship. To turn guests into pipeline assets, you need a systematic approach to CRM tagging, follow-up, and relationship monitoring. This playbook details the exact 30/60/90-day cadence that converts conversations into deals.

The podcast conversation is the beginning, not the end. Most hosts treat publishing as the finish line. The founders building businesses with their podcasts treat it as the starting gun.

A podcast guest who never enters your CRM is a wasted relationship. To build a business with a podcast, you need to treat every guest as a potential client, partner, or referral source — and have a system for tracking that relationship. This is the Podcast-to-Pipeline Playbook.

The CRM Tagging System: Tracking Your Intelligence Assets

When a guest is recorded, they shouldn't just be an episode on your website. They should be a contact record in your CRM. At Aloomii, we use a specific tagging system to make follow-up feel personal and ensure no relationship goes cold:

  • Tag: 'Guest' — identifies the origin of the relationship.
  • Tag: 'Episode Number' — for quick context.
  • Tag: 'Topics Discussed' — so you can follow up with relevant insights later.
  • Tag: 'Mutual Connections' — building social proof for the next check-in.
  • Tag: 'Potential Category' — client, partner, referral source, or investor.

This intelligence layer ensures that your follow-up is always contextually relevant. You're not just 'checking in'; you're continuing a conversation.

The 30/60/90-Day Cadence: Building Long-Term Value

The goal is to maintain top-of-mind awareness without being annoying. This is the cadence that converts:

  • Day 0–7: Thank-you message + episode share. Ask if they'd want anything added to the show notes.
  • Day 30: "The episode is performing well — here's a specific stat." Check in on what they're currently working on.
  • Day 60: Find a reason to add value — an introduction, an article they'd find useful, or a relevant market signal.
  • Day 90: The natural pipeline check. "I've been thinking about what you mentioned regarding [Problem X]. We have a client doing something similar — would a quick call be useful?"

By day 90, you've provided value three times and built peer-level trust over a 45-minute recording. The transition to a sales conversation is natural, not forced.
