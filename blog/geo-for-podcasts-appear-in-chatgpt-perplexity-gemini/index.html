GEO for Podcasts: How to Make Your Episodes Appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini Answers

TL;DR

AI engines index text, not audio. Your GEO potential lives in transcripts, show notes, and episode pages. Build them correctly and episodes keep generating citations for 12-24 months. Five specific changes to make now.

Your podcast transcript is sitting on a server somewhere, doing nothing. If it's not structured for AI citation, it's invisible to the engines your buyers now use for research.

How AI Engines Find Podcast Content

They don't index audio. They index text. Your episode's discoverability in AI-generated answers depends entirely on the text surfaces you build: transcripts, show notes, episode pages, and the structured data you embed in them. A podcast with no text infrastructure is invisible to AI engines.

What GEO Episode Pages Look Like

1. Headers matching natural language queries. Not 'Episode 12: Yat Siu on Web3' but 'What Yat Siu Thinks About Enterprise Web3 Adoption in 2026.' The header is the query.

2. FAQ schema. Machine-readable markup telling AI engines 'here is a question, here is the answer.' Minimum 5 Q&As per episode. Highest-leverage GEO change you can make.

3. Entity markup. Who is the guest, what do they do, what company, what's their expertise. AI engines build associations between entities and topics. Clear entity markup = stronger citation probability.

4. Structured, timestamped transcript. Not a wall of text. Timestamps, speaker labels, clean paragraphs. AI engines reward readability.

5. Internal cross-links. Episode pages linked to related content carry stronger entity signal than orphaned pages.

The Compounding Effect

SEO for audio decays. GEO for structured episode pages compounds. The 10th episode page in a thematic series is more powerful than the first. entity signal is cumulative. Every properly structured episode you publish adds to the authority of everything you've already published.

5 Changes to Make Now

1. Add FAQ schema (5+ Q&As per episode). 2. Build structured transcripts with timestamps. 3. Write headers matching natural language queries. 4. Add entity markup for every guest. 5. Cross-link every episode page to 2-3 related articles.
