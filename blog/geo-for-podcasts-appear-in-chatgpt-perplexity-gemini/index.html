GEO for Podcasts: How to Make Your Episodes Appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini Answers

TL;DR

AI engines don't index audio — they index text. Your podcast's GEO potential lives entirely in your transcripts, show notes, and episode pages. Build them correctly and episodes keep generating citations for 12-24 months. Here are 5 specific changes to make now.

Your podcast transcript is sitting on a server somewhere, doing nothing. If it's not structured for AI citation, it's invisible to the engines your buyers now use for research. Here's how to fix that.

How AI Engines Find Podcast Content

They don't index audio. They index text. Your episode's discoverability in AI-generated answers depends entirely on the text surfaces you build around it: transcripts, show notes, episode pages, and the structured data you embed in them. A podcast with no text infrastructure is invisible to AI engines — which means it's increasingly invisible to B2B buyers doing vendor research.

When a potential client asks Perplexity "who are the best AI sales systems for insurance brokers," the answer is built from text that Perplexity has indexed and can reason about. If your podcast conversation covers that topic but the transcript lives in a raw .docx file on your desktop, it doesn't exist to Perplexity. The GEO opportunity is entirely about making your podcast content machine-readable.

What GEO-Optimized Episode Pages Look Like

A GEO-optimized episode page does five things simultaneously:

1. Answers specific questions directly. Headers should mirror the exact questions buyers ask AI — not "Episode 12: Yat Siu on Web3" but "What Yat Siu Thinks About Enterprise Web3 Adoption in 2026." The header is the query. The content is the answer. AI engines pattern-match on this structure.

2. Includes FAQ schema. Machine-readable markup that explicitly tells AI engines "here is a question, here is the answer." Add a minimum of 5 Q&As per episode, structured around what buyers in your vertical actually ask AI. This is the single highest-leverage GEO change you can make.

3. Has entity markup. Who is the guest, what do they do, what company, what's their known expertise. AI engines build associations between entities and topics. Clear entity markup — "Yat Siu, Co-founder and Chairman of Animoca Brands, Web3 gaming investor" — creates a stronger citation probability when buyers ask about those entities.

4. Has a structured, timestamped transcript. Not a wall of text. A scannable document with timestamps, speaker labels, and clean paragraphs. AI engines reward readability. A raw transcript dump is harder to parse than a structured one, and citation probability drops accordingly.

5. Is internally linked to related content. Episode pages that exist in a content ecosystem — linked to related blog posts, connected to other episodes on the same theme — carry stronger entity signal than orphaned pages.

The Compounding Effect

SEO for audio decays. You get a spike on release, it fades within weeks, and the episode is effectively invisible in search within months. GEO for structured episode pages works differently. An episode page built correctly keeps generating citations in AI searches for 12-24 months — because the content is evergreen, the structure is persistent, and the entity associations strengthen with every additional piece of content you publish around the same topics.

The 10th episode page in a thematic series is more powerful than the first, because the entity signal is cumulative. Every episode you publish that's properly structured adds to the authority of everything you've already published. This is the compounding dynamic that makes podcast infrastructure — not just podcast production — worth investing in.

5 Specific Changes to Make Now

1. Add FAQ schema to every episode page — minimum 5 Q&As per episode, structured around what buyers ask AI about the topic covered.

2. Build a structured transcript, not a raw dump — timestamps, speaker labels, clean paragraphs. AI engines reward readability.

3. Write headers that match natural language queries — not "Episode Summary" but the actual question the guest's answer addresses.

4. Add entity markup for every guest — name, title, company, known expertise, notable work. Build the association explicitly.

5. Cross-link every episode page to 2-3 related articles — reinforcing the topical cluster and strengthening entity signal.
