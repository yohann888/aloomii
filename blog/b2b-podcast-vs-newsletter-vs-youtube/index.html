B2B Podcast vs. Newsletter vs. YouTube: Where Should a Founder Invest First?

TL;DR

For relationship-driven B2B: podcast first. For writing-native founders building a list: newsletter. For product-led businesses: YouTube. Best stack for most B2B founders: podcast + newsletter.

You can't do everything. The founders who win on content pick one channel, go deep, and build the infrastructure to make it compound.

The Honest Comparison

ChannelPipelineTime/moGEORelationships
PodcastHigh8-12 hrsHighVery High
NewsletterMedium4-8 hrsMediumMedium
YouTubeMedium15-20 hrsMediumLow

Podcast: The Relationship Channel

The highest relationship ROI of any channel. Every guest is a warm contact. Every listener who reaches out has already spent 45 minutes with your thinking. With infrastructure, time is manageable. Best for: relationship-driven B2B, professional services, financial, insurance, enterprise.

Newsletter: The List-Building Channel

The best channel for building a direct audience. A newsletter subscriber gave you inbox access. warmer than any social follower. Pipeline impact is medium because newsletters are passive: you're distributing content, not engineering relationships. Best for: founders who think in long-form and are in verticals where thought leadership drives purchase decisions.

YouTube: The Discovery Channel

Highest compounding potential. YouTube is the second largest search engine and great videos rank for years. But time investment is significant and relationship-building potential is lowest. Best for: founders with products that benefit from demonstration.

The Decision Framework

Is your business relationship-driven? Podcast first. Do you think in long-form writing? Newsletter is natural. Does your product benefit from being shown? YouTube. What does your ICP actually consume?

The Combo Play

For most B2B founders: podcast + newsletter. The podcast generates relationships and GEO assets. The newsletter distributes episode summaries and keeps the audience warm. One feeds the other. Add YouTube later when video infrastructure is feasible.
