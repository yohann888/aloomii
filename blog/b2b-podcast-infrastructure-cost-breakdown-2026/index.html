What Does B2B Podcast Infrastructure Actually Cost? A Transparent Pricing Breakdown for 2026

TL;DR

DIY costs $0 cash but 15-20 hrs/week. Agencies run $5K-15K/mo for production only. Done-for-you infrastructure with ICP-targeted guest research, GEO optimization, and pipeline integration: $3,000/mo. One client conversion pays for a full year.

The #1 reason B2B founders don't launch a podcast isn't strategy — it's sticker shock when they start pricing out production. The range is enormous and the options are confusing. This post gives you the real numbers.

The Sticker Shock Problem

Ask a podcast agency for a quote and you'll see anything from $3,000 to $20,000 per month. Ask a freelancer editor and you'll see $200 per episode. Try to do it yourself and discover it takes 15 hours of your week. None of these options is obviously right — and most founders give up before they ever start. The confusion is understandable because most pricing comparisons are apples-to-oranges.

The 4 Models

Model 1 — Full DIY: $0 cash cost, 15-20 hours per week. The hidden cost is your time. At a conservative founder rate of $200/hr, that's $3,000-4,000/mo in opportunity cost, every month, with no ceiling. DIY works when you have more time than money. Most founders don't.

Model 2 — VA-Assisted: $1,500-3,000/mo for editing, show notes, and scheduling. Gets production off your plate but leaves you managing the VA, doing your own guest research, handling your own booking, and building your own distribution. You're still spending 6-8 hours a week. The show notes are functional, not GEO-optimized.

Model 3 — Traditional Agency: $5,000-15,000/mo. Gets you production quality and a reliable output cadence. Doesn't get you ICP-targeted guest research, pipeline integration, or content optimized for AI citation. You're paying for polish on a process designed for celebrity podcasters, not B2B founders building pipeline.

Model 4 — Done-for-You Infrastructure (Aloomii): $3,000/mo. Everything included — ICP-targeted guest research, outreach and booking, production, 8-12 clips per episode, GEO-optimized show notes, multi-platform distribution, monthly pipeline report. Guest research is treated as a pipeline activity, not an admin task.

What You're Actually Paying For

Production quality is a commodity. Any decent freelancer can edit audio. What's scarce — and what determines whether a podcast generates pipeline — is strategic infrastructure: ICP-aligned guest selection, outreach that converts, GEO-optimized episode pages that compound over time, and a system for turning every guest into a warm CRM contact. That's what you can't buy from a production agency.

The ROI Math

One podcast guest who becomes a client at $36K ARR pays for a full year of Aloomii Podcast Infrastructure in the first month. One referral from a podcast guest who becomes a client does the same. The math works when the guest selection is strategic. It doesn't work when you're optimizing for downloads.
