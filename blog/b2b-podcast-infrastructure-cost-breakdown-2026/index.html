TL;DR

B2B podcast production costs range from $0 (DIY, with 15-20 hrs/week of your time) to $15K+/mo (traditional agency). The right model depends on what you're optimizing for. If pipeline is the goal — not downloads — only one model includes the strategic infrastructure to get there: ICP-targeted guest research, GEO-optimized episode pages, and pipeline integration. Aloomii delivers all of it at $3,000/mo.

The #1 reason B2B founders don't launch a podcast isn't strategy — it's sticker shock when they start pricing out production. The range is enormous and the options are confusing. This post gives you the real numbers.

More importantly, it gives you a framework for thinking about what those numbers actually buy — and where most founders make the mistake of optimizing for the wrong metric entirely.

The Sticker Shock Problem

Ask a podcast agency for a quote and you'll see anything from $3,000 to $20,000 per month. Ask a freelancer editor and you'll see $200 per episode. Try to do it yourself and discover it takes 15 hours of your week. None of these options is obviously right — and most founders give up before they ever start.

The confusion comes from comparing things that aren't comparable. A full-service agency at $12,000/mo is not the same product as a freelance editor at $200/episode. An in-house coordinator isn't the same as a strategic podcast infrastructure system. You're not shopping for a commodity — you're trying to figure out what the right system looks like for a B2B founder who's trying to build pipeline, not chase downloads.

Here's the breakdown by model.

The 4 Models

Model 1 — Full DIY: $0 cash, 15-20 hours per week.

The hidden cost is your time. At a conservative founder rate of $200/hr, that's $3,000–4,000/mo in opportunity cost, every month, with no ceiling. You're doing the research, the outreach, the booking coordination, the recording setup, the editing, the show notes, the clip creation, and the distribution. You're learning production on the job. You're managing a system that shouldn't be a job.

DIY works when you have more time than money and are genuinely willing to learn production. Most founders don't have either. More importantly, DIY produces a show, not a system. There's no ICP-targeted guest research, no GEO-optimized episode pages, no pipeline integration. You're building a content asset, not a sales channel.

Model 2 — VA-Assisted: $1,500–3,000/mo.

Editing, show notes, and scheduling are off your plate. You're still spending 6–8 hours a week — on guest research, booking management, distribution oversight, and VA coordination. The show notes are functional, not GEO-optimized. The guest selection is still driven by whoever you happen to know or find manually. You've removed the lowest-skill production tasks, but you haven't built a pipeline system.

Model 3 — Traditional Agency: $5,000–15,000/mo.

Production quality is handled. You have a dedicated editor, a reliable output cadence, professional audio, and likely some social clip work. You're spending 2–4 hours per week. What you don't have: ICP-targeted guest research, pipeline integration, or content optimized for AI citation. The agency was built for celebrity podcasters and media companies — not for B2B founders trying to convert guests into clients. You're paying for polish on a process designed for the wrong goal.

Model 4 — Done-for-You Infrastructure (Aloomii): $3,000/mo.

Everything included. Guest research that's ICP-targeted — your guests are selected because they match your pipeline criteria, not because they have a big following. Outreach and booking. Production. 8–12 clips per episode formatted for TikTok, Reels, LinkedIn, and Shorts. GEO-optimized show notes and episode pages built for AI citation. Multi-platform distribution. Monthly pipeline report that shows you which guests are warm and what next steps look like.

Guest research is treated as a pipeline activity, not an admin task. Show notes are structured for AI engine citation, not just SEO. You spend less than one hour per week. The system runs.

What You're Actually Paying For

Production quality is a commodity. Any decent freelancer can edit audio. What's scarce — and what determines whether a podcast generates pipeline — is strategic infrastructure.

ICP-aligned guest selection. Outreach that converts because the invitation is framed correctly. GEO-optimized episode pages that compound over time instead of spiking on publish day and decaying within weeks. A system for turning every guest into a warm CRM contact with a tracked follow-up cadence.

That's what you can't buy from a production agency. Agencies optimize for listener experience. They build shows that sound good and look professional. That's valuable for media companies and celebrity brands whose revenue model is audience scale. It's less valuable for a B2B founder whose revenue model is client relationships.

The fundamental question isn't "how much does it cost to produce a podcast?" It's "what does it cost to build a podcast that generates pipeline?" Those are different questions with different answers.

The ROI Math

One podcast guest who becomes a client at $36K ARR pays for a full year of Aloomii Podcast Infrastructure in the first month. One referral from a podcast guest who becomes a client does the same.

The math works when the guest selection is strategic. It doesn't work when you're optimizing for downloads. Downloads are a vanity metric for B2B founders. That's the infrastructure. That's what $3,000/mo buys when it's built correctly. The question isn't whether it's worth it. The question is whether you want a show or a sales channel.
