The Real Cost of an SDR in 2026: A Founder’s Honest Breakdown

In 2026, the traditional math of scaling a sales team has fundamentally broken. Hiring a single junior Sales Development Representative (SDR) now costs a founder approximately $120,000 per year when fully loaded ($95k payroll + $25k AI software tax). However, an AI Sales System like Aloomii delivers 24/7 output for just $54,000 per year. This represents a 55% reduction in direct costs while eliminating the 3 to 4 month “dead zone” of negative ROI associated with human ramp times. For B2B founders, the choice is no longer about headcount. It is about capital efficiency and the survival of the unit economic model.

The $120,000 Liability: Breaking Down the Fully-Loaded SDR

Most founders look at a base salary and think they understand their costs. In reality, the base is only the beginning of the hemorrhage. To field a competent human SDR in today’s market, the financial breakdown is stark:

  • Base Salary: $65,000.
  • OTE and Commissions: $15,000.
  • Taxes and Benefits: $15,000.
  • The AI SaaS Tax (The Franken-Stack): $25,000 (Includes Claude for Work, Perplexity Pro, Clay, Sales Nav, and data enrichment).

When you audit these numbers, you are not just paying for a person. You are paying for a massive infrastructure required to keep that person functional.

The ROI Killer: Ramp Time and the 14-Month Turnover Cycle

The hidden cost of human SDRs is not just the monthly burn, but the time-to-value gap.

The Negative ROI Ramp

A human SDR typically requires 3 to 4 months of intensive training before they generate their first qualified meeting. During this period, the company is paying 100% of the costs for 0% of the output. In a fast-moving B2B environment, four months of stagnant pipeline is a death sentence for early-stage growth.

The Tenure Trap

Industry data shows that the average tenure for a junior SDR is now roughly 14 months. By the time a human rep has finally mastered your messaging and hit their stride, they are already looking for a promotion to Account Executive or moving to a competitor for a 10% raise. Founders are essentially paying for a perpetual training program rather than a stable lead generation engine.

From Depreciating Expense to Compounding Asset

Perhaps the most overlooked factor in the AI vs. Human debate is the nature of the investment. When you hire an SDR, you are paying for a depreciating asset. Once they leave, their knowledge, their relationships, and their “feel” for your market walk out the door with them.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a junior SDR cost in 2026? +

Base salary for a junior SDR ranges from $50,000 to $65,000. Add commission, benefits, recruiting fees, the AI toolstack they need to operate, and manager time, and the all-in annual cost typically lands between $100,000 and $130,000.

What is included in the true cost of an SDR? +

The full cost includes base salary, on-target earnings, employer-side benefits (roughly 25% of base), recruiting and onboarding costs, the software they need (Apollo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Outreach), and the time your most senior person spends managing them.

How long does an SDR last at a typical company? +

The average SDR tenure is 14 to 18 months. When an SDR leaves, you restart the recruiting and ramp cycle, which means each departure costs an additional $20,000 to $40,000 in lost productivity and rehiring.

What does an AI SDR cost compared to a human SDR? +

AI sales systems like Aloomii run at $3,500 to $4,500 per month, compared to $10,000 or more per month all-in for a human SDR. The AI system does not require ramp time, does not turn over, and does not need a toolstack on top of its own cost.

Is the ROI on an AI SDR better than a human SDR? +

For most firms, yes. The cost differential alone is significant: $42,000 to $54,000 per year for AI versus $120,000 or more for a human. If meeting volume is comparable, the ROI gap is substantial, and AI systems typically outperform on consistency.
